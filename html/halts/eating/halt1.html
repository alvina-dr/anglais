[Eating] The impact of our food in the planet

The impact of our food on the planet : To begin with, we eat more meat than our body needs. Moreover, to produce a steak you need 4500L of water. We consume about 352,000 tons of meat per year in the world, this production needs 4320 billion liters of water, which is equal to 13,500 Liters of water for a kilo of red meat. Then, according to Greenpeace, cattle breeding is responsible of at least 80% of the disappearance of the Amazon rainforest. Livestock also produces more greenhouse gases than exhausts. If you take all of this information into account, eating meat is really bad for our planet and for our health in the long run! Indeed, cold meats consumption and probably red meat are carcinogenic. Eating too much processed red meat promotes cardiovascular disease, high cholesterol, high blood pressure, osteoporosis and impaired cognitive functions, among others.

In addition, they consume much less water than conventional livestock farming, have high quality protein and have a low risk of transmission of zoonotic disease (avian flu or coronavirus).
