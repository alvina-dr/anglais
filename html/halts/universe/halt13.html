[Universe] Space Habitats

According to theoretical studies carried out by Gerard K. O'Neill and NASA in 1975-1977, space habitats located in space, called space colonies, orbital colonies or space stations, may one day be used to house humans permanently. They will be real cities or autonomous worlds, of varying size, from several thousand to several million individuals. For O'Neill, these space habitats are the best method of colonizing space, more viable than colonizing planets.

The main disadvantage of the colonies located in space is the lack of raw materials that will have to be imported from other planets or asteroids, their advantage is the absence of gravity for much cheaper transport costs. Space habitats can be located in Earth orbit or on Lagrange points to be close to Earth. This project will help overpopulation.
