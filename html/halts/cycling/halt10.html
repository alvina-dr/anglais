[Cycling] Bicycle Infantry

In the end of the 19th century, european countries began to replace cavalry messengers by cycling divisions. By the start of the first World War, each country fighting the war had cycling divisions. France developed the firsts foldable bikes, and each regiment had a cycling unit, meant to perform cross country skirmishing and recon. The Dutch and Belgian, due to the flatness of their country, used bikes mostly to transmit messages and orders.The Italians and Swiss armies used bikes to cross mountain passes where horses couldn't go. In 1937, Japan even deployed 50000 cyclists to invade China! Bicycles were used in later wars,in the Vietnam war or for example in the french resistance during german occupation, which is even now celebrated with “La résistance”, a cycling race taking place in the famous Plateau des Glières.
