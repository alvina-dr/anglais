[Sleep] The lack of sleep

Lack of sleep increases appetite for large portions of high-calorie foods. The fault is the overproduction of ghrelin, appetite hormone.

The consequences of sleep deprivation are most often underestimated. The most recent scientific studies show how a single night of less than 6 hours of sleep has physical and psychological effects on our health and emotions.

A lack of sleep disturbs selective attention, including the difficulty of focusing on one piece of information at a time.

Sleep plays a big role in the phenomenon of memorization, the sorting of new learnings. Lack of sleep prevents this consolidation of memory. Lack of sleep leads to mood disorders and irritability.

Sleep deprivation weakens immune function. A night of sleep that lasts less than 7 hours multiplies by 3 the risk of catching a cold. The risk is multiplied by 5 if you have serious sleep problems.

A sleep duration of less than 7 hours is associated with an increased risk of diabetes. Subjects sleeping less than 6 hours per night are at a high risk of having cardiovascular disease 15% more compared to those with a normal sleep duration

The association between night sleep duration and mortality risk is characterized by a U-curve, with the highest risk in short and long sleepers.

SLEEPING PILLS ARE NOT THE SOLUTION

They alter the quality of sleep and have deleterious effects on health, as revealed in an English study of 100,000 people, divided into two groups, over an average of 7 years. Anxiolytics and sleeping pills were consumed by about a third of the subjects, the others took nothing. “The results are implacable,” says Dr. Patrick Lemoine. “The risk of mortality is almost double in the first group.”
