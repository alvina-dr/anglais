[Sleep] Sleep in Asia (part 2)

Sleep in Korea and Japan is also important but in a very different way.

Japaneseâ€™s sleep lasts 4 to 6 hours a night. Leaving the office before the chief, is against the rules of politeness. Energy drinks are free after 10 pm in large companies.

Falling asleep briefly is tolerated at school, at work and even in parliament. These micro naps are called inemuri, we can translate it by sleeping while being present.

They sleep differently, and their perception of sleep is not the same: it is considered a waste of time, because you have to know how to put your personal interests aside for higher interests.

The books on Japanese sleep are among the best-sellers in bookstores, with titles like "4h of effective sleep" or "the lie of 8h of sleep".

Officially the inemuri (micro nap) is not allowed in classes, but teachers tolerate it, otherwise they could not keep up with the pace of studies.

Sleep deficit is chronic in Japan and Korea, they do not give the same meaning to their need for sleep as we do. They rest in a fragmented way, spread over the day.
