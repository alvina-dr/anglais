[Sleep] Sleep in Asia (part 1)

In China, the pace of economic progress seems to go hand in hand with that of sleep. Sleep time is essential for well-being. It is important for productivity, even when the ultimate goal is to maximize revenues.

People in the Asia-Pacific region seem to have understood this: they spend most of their free time ( well over half ) on personal hygiene, which starts with sleep. The Chinese are the largest sleepers in the Asia-Pacific region, with an average of nine hours of sleep per day.

In all countries in the region except Korea and Japan, people spend a lot of time sleeping. Sleep accounts for more than 70% of free time.

In China, there is a whole Chinese medicine around sleep. The "Qi", what we do during the day and the emotions we feel have an impact on our sleep, which can lead to a disturbance of Qi and therefore organs.

For Chinese medicine, which dates back more than 3,000 B.C. Each organ has a physical and emotional function that maximizes over a two-hour cycle. If this cycle is disrupted by a night awakening, it means that you have a deficient organ.

Sleeping on the right side is the position recommended by traditional Chinese medicine, simply because the blood returns to the liver in this position and provides a calm sleep. This position also makes digestion easier because the stomach more easily drains the fondue you ate two hours earlier to the intestines.

Several studies have found coherence between Chinese business development and their sleep. So sleep has a really important place in Chinese culture.
